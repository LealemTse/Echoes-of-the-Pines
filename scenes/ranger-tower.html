“How did get here without getting caught?”
“I don't know I run as fast as i could”

The door creaks open. The cabin smells of old paper and ozone. Maps cover every inch of the walls, obscured by frantic red marker lines.

A handwritten note is pinned to the center of the main map with a knife.

"They aren't lost. They are being kept. The fog uses the metal as a conductor. If you find the Anchor (The Locket), DO NOT TAKE IT OUT yet. Place it on the map. Align the inscription with the Ley Lines."

You look down. A loose floorboard near the window has been pried up. Something silver glints in the darkness beneath it.

ITEM ACQUIRED: SILVER LOCKET
Inscription: 44°N - The Singing Tree

It's heavy. As soon as you touch it, the static in your ears gets louder. The radio clicks on.

RADIO (104.5):

"You found it. Okay, listen. That Ranger wasn't crazy. He was trying to map the perimeter."

You hold the locket up to the wall map, matching the "Singing Tree" inscription to a red circle on the paper.

When the silver touches the paper, the red marker lines seem to shift. You realize the pattern isn't random. The red circles form a Pentagon around the valley.

Tower 4 isn't a watchtower. It's a fence post. And the locket is a battery.

RADIO (104.5):

"If you remove that locket, you break the circuit. The fog will expand. But if you leave it... the things trapped inside stay trapped. Including the missing people."

The mystery has shifted. You aren't just retrieving an heirloom. You are holding the key to the valley's containment.
